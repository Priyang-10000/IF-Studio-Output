Production Methods
Two primary techniques for growing diamonds in laboratory conditions
CVD
Chemical Vapor Deposition — hydrocarbon gas is ionized, carbon atoms deposit on a seed crystal layer by layer.
Temp: ~900°C
Pressure: 1–27 torr
~65% market share
HPHT
High Pressure High Temperature — mimics natural conditions deep in the Earth using metallic catalyst solutions.
Temp: 1,300–1,600°C
Pressure: 50,000–70,000 atm
~35% market share

Future Outlook 2025–2035
Projected trends shaping the lab diamond industry
📉 Continued Price Decline
Prices expected to fall another 40–60% by 2030 as production efficiency improves. Cost of growing 1ct: from ~$300 today to under $100.
🌱 Renewable Energy Push
Diamond Foundry already runs on 100% renewable energy. Industry-wide net-zero commitments expected by 2030 from major producers.
⚛️ Quantum Tech Boom
NV-center diamonds in quantum computing & sensing expected to be a $2B+ market by 2030. Ultra-pure CVD diamonds are essential.
💍 Bridal Market Shift
Lab diamonds projected to capture 30–40% of the US engagement ring market by 2026, up from 17–20% in 2023.
🔩 Electronics Integration
Diamond semiconductors conduct heat 5× better than silicon. Ultra-wide bandgap properties make them ideal for next-gen power electronics & 6G.
